TOSE CO-FOUNDERS

Minolta Ndlovu

Program Manager

Minolta is an undergraduate student at Bryn Mawr College where she is studying Computer Science and Data Science. Minolta is passionate about building products that give access to resources to marginalized communities. She is also passionate about Computer Science education. At Bryn Mawr College, Minolta is an active advocate for the inclusion of minorities in Computer Science as a Codepath Tech Fellow and as the President of the Girls Who Code club. Minolta was named "The New Face of Tech" by 1,000 Dreams Fund in partnership with Harman, a Samsung company. She is excited to offer her expertise Tose Foundation and help build the next generation of bold leaders in Zimbabwe.

Ruvimbo Taanarwo

Finance Director

Ruvimbo Taanarwo is a student at Wellesley College studying Quantitative Economics. She is passionate about tech-driven innovation with a focus on designing for human needs and is particularly interested in closing the digital divide in areas like education and social software. She is a firm believer in equalizing access to opportunities to all, hence the reason she joined Tose, to create a world in which anyone, regardless of background, has the opportunity to thrive. This matters because the struggle for equality has many fronts, including the education scene. Ruvimbo is honored to join Tose's board in the fight for the education, empowerment, and recognition of low-income students in Zimbabwe.

Chelsea Masikati

Director of Admissions

Chelsea Masikati is a student at Concordia College majoring in Chemistry, with hopes of eventually going to Dental School. She is passionate about impacting the lives of individuals that come from low income backgrounds through healthcare and education. She desires to eventually devote her life towards serving communities in developing countries that do not have access to enough healthcare. A heart for serving others grew in her as a result of her past life experiences that taught her the importance of uplifting others to reach their dreams. Chelsea strongly believes in giving back and Tose Foundation is an opportunity for her to do that to the many children in Zimbabwean communities that lack access to enough resources

Sandra Chimutsipa

Program Associate

Sandra Chimutsipa is a student at St. Olaf College majoring in Psychology and concentrations in International Relations and Management. She is a young lady determined to improve the lives of fellow young people in Zimbabwe through mental health advocacy. Her experiences as an International student has opened her to the importance of equalizing opportunities for all both in academia and the professional world. This has motivated her work in Tose, ensuring that all young people have access to resources and opportunities that enable them to reach greater heights. Sandra finds joy in serving others and hence joined the Tose team in their vision to equalize access of resources to all.
