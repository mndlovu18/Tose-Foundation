Who we are

Tose Foundation is a non-profit organization on a mission to create a level playing field between students in underfunded schools and private, well-established schools in Zimbabwe. The team was born out of a desire to institute change at a grassroots level in Zimbabwe through a combination of education, empowerment and encouraging entrepreneurial innovation. We are committed to introducing our fellow Zimbabweans to a holistic experience including:

  • Educational platforms to develop skills in various industries that our fellows may be interested in
  • Career program that provides fellows with access to internship and job opportunities in and outside Zimbabwe through Resume, LinkedIn and personal branding workshops
  • Access to learning opportunities in Finance and Computer Science, and access to mentors who will help them navigate their career path

Education Disparities in Zimbabwe

Disparities between underfunded schools and private schools

Article by Wadzanayi Mayiseni

In Zimbabwe, I did four years of O’Level at a government high school before moving to a private one for two years of A’Level. One stark difference I noticed upon my transition from Roosevelt to Arundel was the smaller students to teacher ratio. At Roosevelt, we would be roughly 45 students in a class while O’level students at Arundel would not exceed 20 in one class, and 10 for A’level classes. Upon reflection, I appreciate the joys of learning with such great diversity of minds in one classroom but wish I had more time to engage deeply with my peers on class material and establish helpful relationships with my teachers - a privilege I was fortunate to experience at Arundel owing to the smaller student-teacher ratio.

Zimbabwe's education system is now in crisis and its poorly paid teachers are leaving government-run schools in the thousands.

Zimbabwe schools remained closed in 2020 but online learning is restricted to a priviledged few who attend private schools.

The majority of the wealthier portion of the population send their children to independent schools, while the government-run schools are attended by the poorer members of the black population.
